Decision Intelligence Framework

The Bottleneck

Customer support workflows are shackled by "Discoverability Blindness." When agents encounter complex member issues, they lack the technical literacy to identify which SQL queries to execute, or even how to search for the correct diagnostic protocol in the knowledge management system. They are currently stuck reading static articles that provide no guidance on "if-then" execution, leading to stalled cases and inconsistent service.

The Architectural Solution

I am building an intelligent decision framework that maps "member symptoms" to executable diagnostic paths. Instead of searching for keywords, the agent enters the member's issue; the engine then dynamically generates a guided workflow. It tells them exactly which SQL queries to pull, how to interpret the output, and—most importantly—how to troubleshoot if the data returns an error. I am effectively turning the Knowledge Management System from a passive document repository into an active, decision-support engine.
